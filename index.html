FastVidar: End-to-End Real-Time Omnidirectional Depth Estimation
via Direct Fisheye Correlation

Abstract

Depth estimation with fisheye cameras is crucial to the development of omnidirectional vision systems. Existing approaches typically map fisheye images into formats that facilitate stereo epipolar matching, which inevitably sacrificing peripheral visual information and introducing additional distortion. To preserve as much as the visual information, we propose a novel real-time omnidirectional depth estimation system named FastVidar, consisting of three main parts:

(1) A direct fisheye pair depth searching method for images correlation with less field-of-view loss and distortion impact,

(2) A lightweight end-to-end stereo depth estimation network that better balances accuracy and efficiency, achieving real-time inference,

(3) A two-stage training pipeline that combines synthetic dataset pre-training with real-world LiDAR supervision fine-tuning. We conduct an analysis of various binocular fisheye camera placements to guide the design of real-world device for optimal performance, achieving real-time full-field depth estimation directly from raw stereo images. Experimental results demonstrate that FastVidar outperforms baseline methods in both accuracy and efficiency.
